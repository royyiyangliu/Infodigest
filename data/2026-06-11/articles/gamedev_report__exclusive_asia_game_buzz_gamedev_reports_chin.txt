China’s Gaming Market
China’s gaming market is valued at $51.786 billion.
The country had over 683 million players by the end of 2025.
The Steam audience in China reached 42.4 million by the end of 2025. The rest of the world accounts for 89.6 million players.
Client-based PC games (both premium and F2P) account for 22.28% of the total Chinese market, or $11-12 billion.

The legal console market is considerably smaller, estimated at $1.1-1.2 billion in 2025.
Steam accounts for 87% of all MAU in China’s PC/console market. PlayStation is around 6%, Nintendo 5%, and Xbox 2%.
❗️Accurately estimating the share of Chinese platforms and the grey market console segment is difficult. Worth keeping in mind.
As of the end of 2022, the country had over 16.7 million console players (including the grey market), with console revenue at around $2.3 billion for the same period.
There is a reference to the report I’ve published not so long ago in collaboration with Meridian Play.
52% of Wo Long: Fallen Dynasty buyers have Hong Kong PlayStation accounts, which suggests a significant portion are Chinese players.
Average game prices in China are lower than in the West, by 20% to 37%. Worth factoring in when setting prices.
ChinaJoy (over 410,000 visitors in 2025) and Bilibili World (300,000 visitors in 2025) are the main gaming events in China and the best venues for showcasing a game.
Competition at those events is intense, however, so smaller events are also worth considering.
Gaming merchandise is extremely popular in China. Transaction volumes in this category are growing, and in 2025, over 100 million users searched for such products on the platform.
Local Platforms
Heybox is the primary PC key distribution channel in China. TapTap and Sonkwo also have meaningful volume. These platforms should be on any developer’s radar when entering the Chinese market.
For marketing, Heybox, Bilibili, and Douyin are essential. Xiaohongshu (RED) and Weibo are slightly less dominant but still highly significant.
Asia Game Buzz has put together a useful map explaining how Chinese users use different platforms. It shows that no single platform offers all functionality, making a multi-platform presence important.
In terms of DAU and MAU, Bilibili is one of the largest platforms in the country. TapTap, however, is more focused on the gaming audience.
In the first half of 2025, 19 of the 100 fastest-growing channels on Bilibili were gaming-focused.
As elsewhere in the world, curated lists of top games by theme or category are popular in China.
