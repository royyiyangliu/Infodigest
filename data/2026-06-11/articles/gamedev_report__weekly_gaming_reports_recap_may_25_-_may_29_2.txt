Amir Satvat: Gaming Industry Headcount Grew Just 0.6% Over 4 Years
Amir Satvat is the founder of Always Supporting the Gaming Community (ASGC), a community that helps people find work both inside and outside the games industry
.
Per Amir’s data, the gaming industry added 4,000 jobs over four years, a 0.6% increase. At the end of Q1’26, when the report was published, the industry employed just over 754,000 people.
In the US alone, headcount fell 11.5% over the same period.
From 2022 to 2026, the industry hired 52,500 people while over 48,000 lost their jobs.
From 2017 to 2022, the gaming industry grew across nearly every region. Job growth over that period: Brazil +210%, Poland +150%, Sweden +88%, Australia +59%, UK +58%, Canada +48%.
Conservative estimates put new job creation at 150,000-250,000 during that earlier period. From 2022 to 2026: just 4,500.
From 2022 to 2026, around 288,000 people were actively looking for work in the industry: 225,000 recent graduates, 43,000 laid-off workers, and 20,000 career changers and self-taught candidates.
On average, there are around 5 candidates per hire. In the US, the situation is worse: 127,000 job seekers against 12,000 hires. A ratio of 11 to 1.
From 2022 to 2026, 6.4% of all gaming industry workers lost their jobs. North America was hit hardest, with 19.2% of the workforce affected. South America: 11%, Europe: 6.7%, Asia: just 2%.
Amir notes that throughout this period, the industry has had roughly 9,000-14,000 open positions at any given time. But the geography is shifting: opportunities in North America are declining while Europe and Asia are seeing more openings.
Amir projects 11,580 layoffs in the gaming industry in 2026, revised up from an earlier estimate of 10,681.
Unfortunately, the pace of layoffs in 2026 has not slowed. Year-to-date estimates have grown 44%.
North America accounts for 73% of all layoffs. The US leads at 49%, followed by Canada at 24%. Europe accounts for 21%.
The map shows northeastern US states drawing talent toward them, though the majority of open positions in the US remain on the West Coast.

Market Numbers
In April 2026, the US gaming market generated $4.256 billion in revenue, up 3% year-over-year.
Total gaming market revenue for the first four months of 2026 is running 5% ahead of the same period in 2025, driven by growth in both content and hardware sales.
Game content sales grew 2% to $3.837 billion.
Content sales for the first four months of 2026 are running 3% ahead of last year.
US consumer spending on console games grew 21% YoY in April. Subscription spending grew 13%.
Hardware sales grew 34% to $261 million. Nintendo Switch 2 growth more than offset declines in original Switch (-69% YoY), Xbox Series (-43%), and PlayStation 5 (-30%). April was Nintendo Switch 2’s best revenue month since July 2025.
Nintendo Switch 2 led in April by both units and revenue, with PlayStation 5 in second place.
After 66 months on the market, PlayStation 5 is tracking 2% ahead of PlayStation 4 in US unit sales.
Nintendo Switch 2 is running 11% ahead of the original Nintendo Switch after 11 months.
The only declining segment in April 2026 was accessories, which fell 5% to $159 million.
Cases and organizers were up 160% versus last year, driven by Nintendo Switch 2 attach purchases.
Game Sales
❗️Starting April 2026, Circana is combining actual sales figures with projections in its charts. Projected figures are marked in the table.
Under the new methodology, Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream, Pragmata, and Crimson Desert are the top three titles by revenue in April.
Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream earned an estimated $41 million in the US, per Circana’s new methodology that combines digital and physical sales.
Following its launch, consumer spending on physical game copies grew 44% YoY in April to $96 million. Physical sales growth year-to-date (first four months): +9%.
Windrose (#5) and Saros (#9) also had strong starts.
Through the first four months of 2026, Resident Evil: Requiem, Crimson Desert, and MLB: The Show 26 remain at the top of the year-to-date chart.
Among new releases, only Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream has entered the year-to-date rankings.
The mobile market remains stable. MONOPOLY GO!, Royal Match, and Last War: Survival hold their lead. Pixel Flow continues its steady climb and is now at #13 by US revenue.

Sensor Tower: Mobile Market in Q1 2026 - sponsored by PWN Games
❗️All revenue figures are gross. Subscriptions and in-app purchases are included. Sensor Tower data excludes alternative Android stores, as well as ad revenue and payments outside of platforms (D2C).
Mobile Market in Q1’26
IAP revenue continues to grow. In Q1’26, total across all apps reached $43.5 billion, up 9.3% versus Q1’25.
All of that growth, however, is coming from apps. In Q1’26, apps generated $23.1 billion (+17.9% YoY), while games brought in $20.4 billion (+0.5% YoY).
Google Play revenue is growing faster (+14% YoY) than iOS (+6.9% YoY). The picture is different for games specifically: iOS game revenue is falling YoY, while Google Play game revenue is edging up slightly.

Downloads in Q1’26 fell 0.8% YoY to 37.5 billion.
Apps grew 4.9% in Q1’26 (25.6 billion installs), but could not offset the 11.9% YoY decline in game installs (11.9 billion).
Google Play declined by 1.5B downloads. The drop on iOS was far more modest in absolute numbers.
Key Mobile Markets
The US gaming market grew 3% YoY. However, Sensor Tower analysts note that Q1’26 is the second consecutive quarter of declining US revenue, the first time this has happened since Q3-Q4 2020.
European markets are seeing solid growth (not visible on the chart), with UK revenue up 20% YoY in particular.
That said, given the size of the US market, a 3% gain there outweighs a 20% gain in the UK in absolute terms. The US is not a market to write off.
❗️China numbers are covering iOS only because Sensor Tower doesn’t count local Android app stores.
India, the US, and Brazil lead downloads in Q1’26. Only India and Indonesia posted growth, and by just a couple of percentage points.
African countries are posting strong download growth. Worth noting that these figures cover all apps, not games only.
Key Categories in Q1 2026: Game Genres and Non-Gaming Apps
The strongest revenue growth in Q1’26 came from board games (+23% YoY), puzzles (+21% YoY, also one of the largest categories by revenue), and shooters (+12% YoY).
Puzzle growth was driven by Gossip Harbor, Tasty Travels, and Pixel Flow.
Action, RPG, and casino continue to decline in revenue.
The picture of downloads charts is bleak across the board. Everything is falling. Strategy and puzzles are holding up better than others.
Generative AI apps are posting strong growth, with revenue up 174% year over year.
The segment also leads in downloads, though by a smaller margin. Video streaming apps are close behind.
Sensor Tower highlights that the slowdown in AI app download growth may reflect a growing share of users who have already downloaded such apps.

Top Games in Q1 2026
Last War: Survival, Honor of Kings, and Whiteout Survival led Q1 2026 by revenue. The only new entrant in the top 10 was Game for Peace (the Chinese version of PUBG).
The top two titles by revenue each exceeded $500 million for the quarter.
Block Blast! continues to hold the top spot in downloads, marking its second consecutive quarter at number one.
Among the top revenue growth leaders in Q1’26, 7 are Chinese companies. The exceptions: Pixel Flow (Turkey), Dragon Ball Z Dokkan Battle (Japan), and Brawl Stars (Finland).
In download growth, the leaders are Heartopia (which also appeared in the revenue growth chart), Subway Surfers City, and Color Block: Combo Blast (this is NOT Block Blast! and is listed under a different publisher).
Digital Marketing Trends in Q1’26
Digital marketing spend in the US in Q1’26 reached $48 billion, up 15.4% versus Q1’25.
Games rank 6th by marketing spend. US advertisers’ spending on games grew 45.5% YoY.
The picture varies by country. In Japan, for example, games rank first in digital marketing spend and are also growing strongly (+24.3% YoY).
In Turkey, games rank third but are growing 84.2% YoY.
Broadly, digital marketing spend on games in Q1’26 was significantly up year-over-year in almost every country.

game: Germany’s Gaming Industry has more development studios but fewer employees in 2026 - sponsored by PWN Games
The number of game development companies in Germany grew from 917 in 2025 to 956 in 2026, an increase of 4%.
The strongest growth came from development studios, up 6% YoY to 474 companies.
Companies that both develop and publish their own projects grew 3% to 427.
The number of publishers grew 2% to 55 companies.
Since the federal game funding program was announced, the number of new companies in the country has grown by 50% over four years. Following the program’s end, the company count fell 4% YoY in 2025.
